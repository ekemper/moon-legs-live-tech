## Key Considerations When Choosing

### **1. Driver Size**
- **12"**: Ultra-portable, tight punch, limited deep bass (45+ Hz)
- **15"**: Balanced portability and output (40-42 Hz)
- **18"**: Standard for most pro applications (32-40 Hz)
- **21"**: Maximum output for large venues (23-30 Hz)

### **2. Power Ratings**
- **Entry-Level**: 1000-1200W (adequate for small venues)
- **Professional**: 1500-2000W (most live applications)
- **Tour-Grade**: 3000W+ (large venues, festivals)

### **3. SPL Requirements**
- **Small Venues (<200 people)**: 130-132 dB sufficient
- **Medium Venues (200-500)**: 133-136 dB recommended
- **Large Venues (500+)**: 136-142 dB required
- **Stadium/Festival**: 140+ dB necessary

### **4. Cardioid vs. Omnidirectional**
- **Cardioid**: Reduces stage bleed, better for theaters and installations, more expensive
- **Omnidirectional**: Standard coverage, more energy, lower cost

### **5. Portability vs. Output**
- Consider weight for mobile applications
- One person can reasonably handle 70-90 lbs
- Heavier units typically require 2+ people or equipment

### **6. Budget Allocation**
- **Budget**: $500-900 (entry-level, small venues)
- **Mid-Range**: $900-1,500 (most prosumer applications)
- **Professional**: $1,500-2,500 (serious touring/rental, large venues)

## Maintenance and Care Tips

1. **Transport**: Always use proper cases or covers
2. **Power**: Use appropriate power distribution, avoid generator noise
3. **Cables**: Use quality XLR cables, check for ground loops
4. **Placement**: Avoid corners unless bass-trapping, elevate when possible
5. **DSP**: Start with manufacturer presets, adjust for room
6. **Inspection**: Check drivers regularly for damage, listen for distortion
7. **Firmware**: Keep DSP firmware updated for best performance
